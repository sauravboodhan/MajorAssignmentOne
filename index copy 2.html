The Start of Surrealism

Surrealism Origins

The Surrealist art movement began in the late 1910s, originating during a War-torn world. The artists of this moevement sought to liberate their minds & human experiance from the oppression of rationalism & reality. With this, the art pieces produced by some notable artists of this time have explored the subconcious, flamboyant, mysterious, and often, if not, fantastical elements of one's creativity.

Surrealism Manifestos & Influences

The poets, Yvan Goll & André Breton, are known to be the leaders that brought the start to the Surrealism movement, producing four manifestos, known as the Surrealism Manifestos. Within these manifestos, the poets praised the subconcious, dreams, and psychic automatism, all while refuting the idea of rationality. The Dada movement gave great inspiration in creating these manifestos & the Surrealism movement, with similar ideals about defying reasoning & logic.
